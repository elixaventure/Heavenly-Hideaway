Explore Every Benefit in Depth

"Within two sessions, the visible difference in swelling is something most clients never expected to see so quickly."

Fluid retention occurs when the lymphatic system cannot drain excess fluid fast enough. Manual Lymphatic Drainage physically moves this trapped fluid using precisely sequenced, feather-light strokes that guide it toward the nearest functioning lymph nodes where it is properly processed and eliminated.

Unlike regular massage which works on muscle tissue, MLD operates in the superficial tissue just beneath the skin — exactly where lymph vessels are located. The pressure used is extraordinarily light, which is precisely why it works: too much pressure collapses the vessels; the correct pressure opens them.

What the Research Says

Systematic reviews of randomised controlled trials confirm MLD as an effective treatment for lymphedema — particularly following breast cancer surgery.

"70% of your immune system is associated with lymphatic tissue — making lymphatic health the foundation of immune health."

Your lymphatic system IS your immune system. Lymph nodes filter pathogens and toxins while lymphocytes travel through your lymphatic vessels to reach sites of infection. When sluggish, this immune traffic slows — pathogens linger and recovery takes longer.

By restoring healthy lymph flow, MLD allows immune cells to circulate efficiently, enabling your body's natural defences to perform at full capacity. Many regular clients report significantly fewer illnesses after establishing a treatment routine.

"Clients consistently describe the feeling after MLD as 'lighter' — as if something heavy has been lifted from inside them."

Every cell in your body produces metabolic waste — lactic acid, carbon dioxide and cellular debris — which must be collected and removed by the lymphatic system. When sluggish, this waste accumulates causing fatigue, brain fog, skin problems and general unwellness.

MLD accelerates waste removal by stimulating lymph flow up to 10 times its resting rate during skilled treatment. After a session, clients are encouraged to drink plenty of water — increased bathroom visits are a sign the body is successfully flushing mobilised waste.

Surgery interrupts lymphatic pathways. The body responds with inflammation and fluid accumulation — natural but which, if unmanaged, leads to prolonged swelling, discomfort, fibrosis and compromised results.

MLD redirects lymph fluid away from the surgical site via alternative pathways, dramatically reducing post-operative swelling, preventing seromas, softening scar tissue and reducing infection risk. Lisa's Post-Surgery Programme typically begins from 72 hours post-procedure with surgical clearance.
